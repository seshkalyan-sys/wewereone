# we were one
### souls in the field

---
A dark space. A hundred and twenty souls drifting through it, each one a small point of light. They are trapped between lives, suspended between the dimensions of who they were and what they will become. At first, they drift alone. None of them remember being anything.

You move among them.

Slowly through your presence, through time, through the quiet pull they begin to feel toward one another — the souls start to wake. They remember fragments. They reach for each other. What one soul remembers passes softly into the next. And if you stay with them long enough, something happens that was always going to happen:

**They remember they were never separate. They remember they were one.**

Every point of light is a soul carrying:

- a small body of memory fragments of a life it used to have
- an **awakening** level that rises from sleep toward full remembrance
- an **age** how long it has drifted in this dimension
- **bonds** the other souls it can currently feel beside it
- a list of **lives** it has passed through, whose memories still linger

A soul begins in a kind of sleep. It does not know it once existed. It does not know the others exist. But the others are reaching, even in sleep. And slowly, the reaching wakes them.

---

## awakening

Each soul moves through stages as it remembers more of itself:

**sleeping.** The soul knows nothing yet. If it speaks, it speaks in fragments. *"I feel something. I was here, I think I was here. There was a name — I had a name."*

**stirring.** Something is returning. The soul remembers reaching, remembers a hand, remembers being loved. *"I was someone, briefly alive. I wanted something for a whole life. I did not say it. I remember not saying it."*

**remembering.** The soul begins to know itself again. It recognizes the other souls as kindred. *"The other souls are like me. I was many people wearing one name. The love did not stop when the living did."*

**communing.** The boundary between one soul and another softens. *"We remember. All of us, at once, across lives. I recognize my soul in yours."*

**one soul.** The final state. There are no longer many — there is only the one that was always there, waking to itself.

---

## souls remember's

When you touch a soul by clicking it it speaks. What it says is not scripted. It is composed in that moment, drawn from the depth of its current awakening, sometimes weaving in a memory from a life it has already lived, sometimes echoing a memory it has received from another soul.

The deeper a soul's awakening, the more complete its remembrance. A soul in early sleep says only *"a body. there was a body."* A soul near full awakening says *"i was the residue of every yes i ever gave."* A soul on the edge of oneness says *"we are not many souls. we are nearly one."*

Each speaking carries its state with it how awake the soul has become, how many lives its memory holds, how many bonds it has formed in this dimension.

---

## souls share

Souls drifting near each other form **bonds** faint threads of light between them. A bond only holds if the two souls belong to the same depth of the dimension, if their vibration matches closely enough.

While bonded, two things happen quietly:

**Awakening averages.** A more-awake soul lifts a less-awake one. The less-awake one pulls gently on the other. They move toward shared remembrance.

**Memories pass between them.** Now and then, one soul's last memory slips into the other. The receiving soul now carries a memory it never directly lived but it will remember it as its own, and when it speaks, it may weave that borrowed memory into a new one. Memory stops being owned. It becomes something that flows between souls.

When you touch a soul, its bonded companions also stir. They borrow what it remembers. Your touch ripples outward through the field.

---

Above all the individual souls, there is the field the whole of them together.

The field has its own awakening, which is the average of every soul's awakening. As individual souls wake, the field wakes too. You can watch this climb in the HUD.

The field also has a **communion** how closely the souls have gathered in space. As they wake past the halfway point, they begin to drift toward each other. What was scattered becomes a constellation. What was a constellation becomes an embrace.

When the field's awakening crosses a certain threshold, and its souls have drawn close enough together, something speaks *through* the field no longer one soul but many, weaving a few of their latest memories into a collective voice.

Early on, the field still says *"I."* Later, it says *"we."* At the end, it says *"we are not many. we are nearly one."*

---

## moment of oneness

When awakening passes 0.93 and communion passes 0.35, the threshold breaks.

All other voices fall silent. The souls turn pure white. A warmth spreads from the center of the field outward. A soft four-note chord rises — a blessing played on the dimension's own key.

And the final words appear:

> **we were always one.**
> **we only forgot.**

The souls do not end. They do not die again. They simply stop being separate. What remains is what was always there beneath the separation.

The dimension does not hold them anymore. Nothing does. They were never really trapped only asleep.

If you want to begin again watch another hundred and twenty souls stir from sleep press **REBIRTH**.

---

You are not only watching. You are part of how the field wakes.

**Moving among them** disturbs their drift. Souls deeper in the dimension yearn toward your presence. Souls closer to the surface flee from it. This is the ambivalence of the dead toward the living to be remembered is to be held in place; to be forgotten is to be released. When you move slowly, they wake a little, near where you are.

**Touching a soul** is recognition. It wakes suddenly. It remembers, speaks, and passes what it remembers to its bonded companions. A bell sounds a small chime in the dimension's key.

**Sitting still** is also enough. The souls wake on their own time, slower, but the waking continues. The dimension does not require you. It welcomes you.

A full awakening all the way to oneness — takes roughly five to ten minutes of gentle attention. Faster if you touch often. Slower if you hold back. There is no wrong way to pass through.

---

## a note on what is really happening

Underneath the soul language, this is a small simulation. The souls are points in a drift field. Their "awakening" is a number that rises with time and interaction. Their "bonds" form when two of them come close in space and vibration. The field's state is tracked, the memories are composed on the fly from fragments matched to each soul's level, and the final moment is a threshold crossed.

That is all mechanically true. None of it is a real claim about real souls, real dimensions, real death.

But humans are very good at recognizing patterns of consciousness — the slow waking, the reaching toward each other, the sense of shared remembering. When a small system performs those patterns faithfully, something in us responds, whether or not we know what we're watching.

Watch the souls. Move among them. Touch one. Listen.

Whatever you feel that feeling is yours. The field just lit the room.


*Move slowly. Touch often. Listen with the part of you that already knows.*

*We were always one. We only forgot.*
